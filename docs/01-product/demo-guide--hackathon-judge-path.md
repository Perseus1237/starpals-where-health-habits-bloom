# StarPals Hackathon Judge Path

## Goal

Use this guide to present StarPals as one connected user journey: a care plan becomes a child-safe quest, the child completes the quest because it has meaning, the parent sees a calm recap, and the care team gets useful signal.

The demo should make four things obvious in the first two minutes:

- The problem is real and painful for kids, parents, and care teams.
- The AI is doing useful work, not just adding decoration.
- The software works and can be opened, clicked, and verified.
- The product can become a cross-condition pediatric engagement layer.

## Recommended Talk Track

**One-sentence pitch:** StarPals turns pediatric care plans into child-safe daily quests, so kids feel like heroes instead of patients, parents stop being the reminder system, and care teams get useful signal between visits.

**Target user:** a child age 7-13 with recurring care routines, plus the parent or guardian who supports those routines and the care team that needs between-visit context.

**Core pain:** pediatric chronic care is not only an adherence problem. It is a daily meaning, motivation, and family-friction problem.

## Click Path

1. Start on `/`.
2. Click **Begin the child demo**.
3. Enter a child name, choose a StarPal, accept or edit the pet name, and enter the habitat.
4. Complete at least three Care Cards: water, breathing, and medicine.
5. Open the personalized story after two completed quests.
6. Show the constellation button after three completed quests.
7. Open the parent dashboard.
8. At the parent gate, answer `7`.
9. Show today's recap, week view, parent controls, care-team notes, and staged AI drafts.
10. Open the care-plan builder.
11. Click **Import** to stage EMR items.
12. Click **Generate AI draft** to show chart-to-quest draft suggestions.
13. Click **Send to parent review**.
14. Return to the landing page and use the bottom judge path section to summarize the product thesis.

## Do Not Overclaim

- Do not claim StarPals diagnoses, prescribes, triages, or replaces clinical care.
- Do not claim the current demo has production EMR integration.
- Do not claim open social networking for children.
- Do not claim clinical outcome improvement until validated in pilots.

## Strong Closing

StarPals starts as a magical habit loop for children, but the wedge is larger: it can become the child engagement operating system for pediatric care plans. The child gets meaning, the parent gets calm, and the care team gets signal.
